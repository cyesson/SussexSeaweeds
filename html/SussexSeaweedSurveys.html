Sussex Seaweeds

Chris Yesson & Ian Tittley

10th Sept 2025

Background

Phycologist Dr Ian Tittley has walked the Sussex coastline recording the seaweeds present in every 1 kilometre section of the shoreline (a geographic unit called a monad). This page showcases the results of these surveys conducted betweek 2021 and 2025.

Data summary

Ian has recorded 6332 observations of 306 different seaweeds at 254 sites (1km2 monads) in Sussex from Chichester to Rye. The sites have been divided into coastal (along the sea shore), estuarine (along rivers no more than 5km from the sea) and offshore (not accessible on foot even at a low spring tide). The interactive table below lists all species observed, sorted by the number of sites the species are present. Click a column heading to sort on that column, type in the search/filter box to filter table by the presence of a keyword (e.g. typing Ochrophyta will show only brown seaweeds). The green seaweeds Blidingia and Ulva are the most common. The most common red seaweeds are Gelidium pusillum and Porphyra purpurea, while the brown seaweed is Fucus vesiculosus is also in the top 10.
